Connections

Real-world, text-to-world, and text-to-text connections found in “Take Us to Your Chief.”

Alcohol Usage in Indigenous Communities

Real World

In this story, the usage of alcohol is heavily normalized and even seen as humorous at times. However, it's not something that should be treated as fun and games. For Indigenous people, alcohol was used as a coping mechanism for dealing with trauma. This created serious addiction to the point where alcohol was seen as a normal thing/stereotype for Indigenous people.

Text to World

Text to World

The aliens mistaking the men's silence as "meta-communication" is reflective of how people misrepresent Indigenous culture and tradition in real life.

Unlike the story where the men are misrepresented in a positive way, being seen as sophisticated, in real life, Indigenous traditions and people were seen as "wild," and were generally viewed in a negative light.

Text to Text

Text to Text

The stories "Take us to Your Chief" and "Shinny Game Melted the Ice" both showcase how close/strong bonds can help overcome difficult challenges. For the three men, their bond allowed them to find comfort in a new environment. For the brothers, their bond helped the main character reconnect with his birth family despite being away for so long. In both stories, close relationships act as a source of stability and identity.
